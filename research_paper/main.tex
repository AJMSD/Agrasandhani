\title{Agrasandhani: Smart Gateway Trade-offs for Stable IoT Dashboards under Last-Hop Impairment}

\author{Aman Jain}
\affiliation{
    \institution{University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign}
    \city{Champaign}
    \state{Illinois}
    \country{USA}
}
\email{aman18@illinois.edu}

\begin{abstract}
A smart gateway can reshape what an IoT dashboard displays under degraded last-hop conditions, but the benefit depends on which operator-visible property is prioritized. This paper evaluates Agrasandhani, an MQTT-to-WebSocket gateway that supports fixed-window batching, latest-per-sensor compaction, and last-known-good state exposure. Using replicated experiments on bounded Intel Berkeley Research Lab traces and a smaller Array of Things validation slice, the study compares V0 (naive forwarding), V2 (batching plus compaction), and V4 (V2 plus last-known-good semantics) under clean, bandwidth-capped, lossy, delay/jitter, and outage scenarios. In the Intel primary matrix, V2 and V4 reduce downstream frames during a five-second outage from 116 in V0 to 4 and 6, respectively, but increase clean-path p95 latency from 31.9\,ms to 276.2\,ms and 607.7\,ms. V4 preserves visible state during outage, but at substantially higher age. The smart variants do not reduce downstream payload bytes in this setup, and the tested adaptive controller does not materially outperform fixed-window batching. The resulting conclusion is therefore narrow: gateway-side pacing improves dashboard continuity and rendering stability under last-hop impairment when continuity is valued over minimum latency, but the trade-off is older visible state and no blanket byte-volume benefit.
\end{abstract}
